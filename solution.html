Proposed Solution

Reducing Learning Barriers Caused by Expensive Agricultural Machines

HOME SOLUTION ABOUT

Combined Virtual Simulation Tools

The proposed solution is the use of combined virtual simulation tools to teach agricultural and fishery machine operation. These simulations allow learners to explore how machines function without physically owning or operating real equipment.

For example, a virtual tractor simulation can demonstrate how land is prepared, while a digital harvester simulation shows proper harvesting techniques. In fishery, simulations can present boat navigation and equipment handling.

Poster-Based Learning Integration

Each virtual simulation is supported by an educational poster that explains the machine’s purpose, main parts, and real-life applications. Posters provide visual guidance that helps learners understand machines step by step.

By combining posters and simulations, students gain both theoretical knowledge and practical understanding, even without direct access to costly machines.

Application in Agriculture and Fishery Arts

This solution can be applied in classrooms, training centers, and community learning spaces. Students can develop confidence and skills through simulations before handling real machines when necessary.

As a result, learning becomes more accessible, safer, and less dependent on owning expensive agricultural equipment.
