High Cost of Machines

We chose Agriculture and Fishery Arts (AFA) because it involves machinery in farming and fishing. Machines improve productivity but their high cost makes them difficult to afford, especially for small farmers.

Summary / Background

Modern farming and fishing rely heavily on machines like tractors, harvesters, and fishing boats. However, their high purchase and maintenance costs limit access for small-scale farmers. This creates inequality, reduces efficiency, and makes farming less sustainable.

Main Problem

The central issue is the unaffordability of modern machines. Farmers and fisherfolk cannot easily buy or maintain equipment, which leads to reduced efficiency and higher production costs.

Root Cause Analysis

Machines are costly due to advanced technology, importation, and maintenance expenses. Without government subsidies, cooperative ownership, or affordable financing, farmers struggle to access modern tools.
