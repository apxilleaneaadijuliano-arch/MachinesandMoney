Agriculture and Fishery Arts (AFA)

AFA is a TLE learning area that teaches students farming, fishing, and food-processing skills. These lessons rely on machines to help students understand real-world agricultural systems.

Main Problem

The high cost of machines limits student exposure to real equipment. Schools often cannot afford enough machines, causing students to rely only on theory instead of hands-on learning.

Examples include tractors, food processors, fishing tools, and irrigation machines that students rarely get to use.

Root Cause Analysis

Machines are expensive due to advanced technology, import costs, and maintenance expenses. As Grade 8 students, our group realized that borrowing machines from partnerships may not always be approved, making learning access unreliable.
