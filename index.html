Modern agriculture and fishery have evolved to rely on advanced machines for planting, harvesting, food processing, and preservation. These machines improve efficiency and food quality, but they come with high purchase and maintenance costs.

Because of this, small farmers are often left behind, forced to rely on manual labor and outdated tools, which leads to lower productivity and unequal opportunities.

The central problem is the high cost of agricultural and fishery machines. Farmers and fisherfolk struggle to buy, repair, or maintain equipment, which increases production costs and limits income.

This problem affects not only individuals but also food supply, economic growth, and sustainability in agricultural communities.
