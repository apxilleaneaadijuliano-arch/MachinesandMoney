A Root Cause Analysis on the High Cost of Machines and its Impact on Farmers

Learning Area Selected

Agriculture and Fishery Arts focuses on real-world skills in farming, fishing, and food production. Machines play a vital role in improving efficiency and productivity within these industries.

However, the high cost of machines limits access for many farmers and fisherfolk, especially those in small-scale and rural communities.

Background

Modern agriculture relies on tractors, harvesters, and processing machines. These tools reduce labor and increase output, but they require significant financial investment.

Main Problem

The unaffordability of machines prevents farmers from modernizing their work, leading to lower productivity and income.

Root Cause

Advanced technology, import costs, maintenance expenses, and lack of financial support all contribute to the high price of agricultural machines.
