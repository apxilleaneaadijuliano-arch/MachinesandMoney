Agriculture and Fishery Arts (AFA)

Agriculture and Fishery Arts (AFA) is a TLE learning area that focuses on developing students’ skills in farming, fishing, and food processing. It prepares students for real-life applications by teaching proper techniques, safety, and the use of machines.

Machines play a major role in modern agriculture and fisheries. Through AFA, students learn how these machines improve efficiency, accuracy, and productivity in real-world settings.

Main Problem: High Cost of Machines

The high cost of machines limits students’ opportunities to experience hands-on learning. Many schools cannot afford enough equipment, which causes students to rely mostly on theoretical lessons instead of actual machine operation.

As a result, students may struggle to fully understand how machines work and how they are safely used in agricultural and fishery practices.

Examples of expensive machines include tractors, harvesters, food-processing equipment, irrigation systems, and fishing tools that are often unavailable for regular student use.

Root Cause Analysis

The main root cause of this problem is the high market price of machines. These costs are influenced by advanced technology, import fees, and maintenance expenses.

During our evaluation, our group realized that relying on school partnerships to borrow machines may not always be approved. This makes access to equipment uncertain and limits consistent learning for students.
