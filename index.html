About the Learning Area

Agriculture and Fishery Arts (AFA) is a Technology and Livelihood Education (TLE) learning area that teaches students practical skills related to farming, fishing, food processing, and agricultural technology. It helps students understand how food is produced and how machines are used in real agricultural systems.

In AFA, machines such as tractors, harvesters, food processors, and farming tools are important because they improve efficiency and accuracy. Learning how these machines work prepares students for real-life applications and future livelihoods.

Main Problem: High Cost of Machines

The main problem identified in this Root Cause Analysis is the high cost of machines used in Agriculture and Fishery Arts. These machines are expensive to buy, maintain, and repair, making them difficult to access in school settings.

As Grade 8 students, we often learn about machines only through pictures, videos, or discussions. Due to limited school resources, students rarely experience actual machine operation, which affects hands-on learning and skill development.

Impact on Students

Because schools cannot always provide expensive agricultural machines, students struggle to fully understand how these tools work. Learning becomes more theoretical than practical, which reduces engagement and confidence.

This situation limits students’ exposure to real-life agricultural practices and makes it harder for them to connect lessons to real-world applications in farming and fishery.

Root Cause Analysis

The root cause of this problem is the high market price of agricultural machines. Machines are costly due to advanced technology, importation fees, maintenance expenses, and limited availability.

During our group evaluation, we realized that simply requesting access to machines through school partnerships may not be reliable, as such requests can be declined due to budget limitations. This made us understand the need for a more realistic and student-centered solution.
