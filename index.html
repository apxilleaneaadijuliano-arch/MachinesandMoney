TLE Learning Area Selected

Agriculture and Fishery Arts (AFA) is a learning area that focuses on practical skills related to farming, fishing, food processing, and agricultural technology. It prepares students to understand real-life agricultural systems and how food is produced, processed, and distributed.

In modern AFA practices, machines such as tractors, harvesters, rice mills, irrigation systems, and fishing boats are essential. These machines help increase efficiency, reduce manual labor, and improve production quality.

Background / Summary

Over the years, agriculture and fishery have become increasingly dependent on machinery. For example, tractors allow farmers to prepare large areas of land faster, while mechanical harvesters reduce crop loss and save time. In fishery, motorized boats and processing equipment help increase catch and preserve products.

However, these machines are often expensive to purchase and maintain. Small-scale farmers and fisherfolk, especially in rural areas, struggle to access such technology, leading to unequal opportunities and slower development.

Main Problem

The main problem addressed in this RCA is the high cost of agricultural and fishery machines. Many farmers and fisherfolk cannot afford to buy equipment or pay for repairs, fuel, and spare parts.

Because of this, they rely on manual labor or outdated tools, which lowers productivity, increases physical strain, and reduces income over time.

Root Cause Analysis

The high cost of machines is caused by several factors, including advanced technology, importation expenses, high fuel prices, and costly maintenance. Many machines are imported, making them more expensive due to taxes and shipping fees.

In addition, the lack of financial support, subsidies, and shared access programs makes it difficult for small farmers to modernize their practices, slowing overall agricultural progress.
