About the Learning Area

Agriculture and Fishery Arts (AFA) teaches students practical skills in farming, fishing, and food processing. It helps students understand how machines are used in real agricultural systems.

Machines like tractors, harvesters, and irrigation tools improve efficiency and allow students to experience hands-on skills for future real-life applications.

Main Problem: High Cost of Machines

The high cost of machines in AFA affects Grade 8 students because schools often cannot provide enough equipment. Students rely on limited classroom resources, which reduces practical learning opportunities.

This problem prevents students from fully understanding machine operation, which can reduce engagement, confidence, and skill development.

Root Cause Analysis

The main cause is the high price of machines due to advanced technology, import fees, and maintenance costs. During our evaluation, we realized that asking for school partnerships to borrow machines may not be reliable.

Therefore, students need a realistic solution that allows learning without owning expensive machines while still practicing real-life skills.
