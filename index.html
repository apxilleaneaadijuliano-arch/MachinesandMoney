Introduction

Agriculture and Fishery Arts (AFA) is a TLE learning area that teaches students practical skills in farming, fishing, and food processing. Modern agriculture relies heavily on machines, but these are expensive, limiting students' hands-on experience and learning opportunities.

Root Cause Analysis (High Cost of Machines)

The high cost of machines prevents students from using them regularly. Advanced technology, import fees, and maintenance make equipment unaffordable for schools. As a result, students often learn only the theory, not the practical skills needed to operate machinery safely and effectively.

Group 5 — AFA Root Cause Analysis

Root Cause Analysis

The main issue is unaffordability. Machines are costly because of manufacturing, import taxes, and lack of subsidies. Without support, farmers use older tools, which lowers productivity and income.

This problem affects not only individual farmers but also food supply and community growth. Understanding the root causes helps us find practical solutions that can improve agriculture for everyone.
